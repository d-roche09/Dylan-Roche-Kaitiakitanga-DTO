Kauri Dieback is a disease that is threatening the life of all Kauri trees in New Zealand. Tane Mahuta is among them and is the oldest kauri tree in the world named after the Māori God of the Birds and the Forest. The spores of the disease is spread on our shoes, on our pets feet, and on tramping gear that we use when exploring the bush. The spores attach themselves to roots where they block the trees food and water resulting in the tree dying.
