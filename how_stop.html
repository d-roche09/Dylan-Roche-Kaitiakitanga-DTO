Kauri Dieback

How to stop it?

What is being done?

The groups who are leading the charge for the protection of Kauri Trees are the Ministry for Primary Industries (MPI) and the Department of Conservation (DOC). They have put specific checkpoints along many walks in New Zealand where you must clean shoes and gear and have specifically marked tracks in order to reduce the spreading of the disease.

What can you do?

❮ Back

One of the main concerns with Kauri Dieback is if you have one in your own backyard!

The best thing you can do is make sure you keep your shoes clean, especially if you have just been on a tramp or walk in the bush. You should also make sure to avoid the roots, the place where the disease attacks.

Make sure you also know what Kauri Dieback looks like, if you see your tree is showing some symptoms and wish to remove it make sure to contact your council for about the matter.
